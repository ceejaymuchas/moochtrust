Overview of Our Work

MOOCH Foundation’s projects are designed to take a holistic approach to child development by addressing interconnected challenges such as hunger, lack of education, poor sanitation, water scarcity, and inadequate infrastructure. Rural children face compounded disadvantages that require coordinated and sustained interventions rather than short-term solutions.

Our project model emphasizes community participation, accountability, and alignment with government development frameworks. By working closely with schools, parents, traditional leaders, and local authorities, we ensure that our initiatives respond to real needs and remain sustainable long after initial implementation.

1. School Feeding Programme

The School Feeding Programme aims to combat hunger and malnutrition among children attending rural primary and secondary schools. Many children in remote areas attend school on empty stomachs, which negatively affects concentration, attendance, and academic performance. MOOCH Foundation provides nutritious meals that support both physical growth and cognitive development.

This programme prioritizes locally sourced food where possible, supporting smallholder farmers and strengthening local food systems. By integrating nutrition education and community involvement, the programme contributes to improved health outcomes and increased school retention rates, particularly among vulnerable children.

2. School Uniforms & Learning Materials Initiative

Lack of proper school uniforms and learning materials is a significant barrier to education in rural Zimbabwe. Children are often excluded from school or attend irregularly due to poverty-related challenges. This initiative provides uniforms, shoes, stationery, and basic learning materials to ensure children can attend school with dignity and confidence.

By reducing the financial burden on families, this project directly supports government efforts to improve access to education and reduce dropout rates. The initiative also promotes equality by ensuring that no child is stigmatized due to their socio-economic background.

3. Dignity for Girls – Sanitary Wear Programme

Menstrual health remains a major challenge for girls in rural communities, often leading to absenteeism and school dropouts. The Dignity for Girls Programme provides sanitary wear, underwear, and menstrual health education to school-going girls, enabling them to attend school consistently and with dignity.

This programme addresses gender inequality in education and aligns with national priorities on gender empowerment, health, and education. By creating safe spaces for dialogue and awareness, MOOCH Foundation helps break cultural stigmas and supports the overall well-being of adolescent girls.

4. Clean Water & Sanitation Projects

Access to clean and safe drinking water is essential for child health and development. In many rural communities, children walk long distances to fetch unsafe water, which exposes them to waterborne diseases. MOOCH Foundation implements borehole drilling, water purification systems, and sanitation awareness programmes in schools and communities.

These projects contribute to improved health outcomes, reduced disease burden, and increased school attendance. Water committees are trained to manage and maintain infrastructure, ensuring sustainability and community ownership of resources.

5. Rural School Construction & Rehabilitation

Many rural schools operate under unsafe and overcrowded conditions, including dilapidated classrooms and inadequate sanitation facilities. MOOCH Foundation works to construct new classrooms, rehabilitate existing structures, and improve school environments to support quality learning.

This project aligns with national education infrastructure development goals and supports the creation of safe, inclusive, and child-friendly learning spaces. By engaging communities in construction efforts, the programme promotes ownership, skills transfer, and long-term maintenance.

Sustainability & Community Engagement

Sustainability is central to all MOOCH Foundation projects. Community involvement, transparency, and collaboration with government institutions ensure that projects continue to benefit children beyond initial implementation. Monitoring and evaluation mechanisms are integrated into each project to measure impact and guide continuous improvement.
